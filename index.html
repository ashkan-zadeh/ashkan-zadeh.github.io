About Me

I'm a PhD researcher at Queensland University of Technology (QUT), working at the intersection of human-centred AI (HCAI), explainable AI (XAI), and natural language processing (NLP)‚Äîall with a mission to make automated vehicles more transparent, intuitive, and aligned with human expectations.

My background spans mechanical and control engineering, where I first got hooked on designing robotic systems through CAD modelling. During my Master's, I shifted gears toward autonomous vehicle control and AI, laying the groundwork for the human-AI collaboration I explore today. Now, in my PhD, I'm focused on creating systems that can not only make decisions but also explain their behaviour in a way that people can easily understand and trust.

My research blends AI modelling and NLP techniques to generate natural-language explanations for automated driving behaviour, helping bridge the gap between complex machine decisions and human interpretation.

Thesis: "A Model for Generating Human-Centric Explanations for Automated Vehicles"

Supervisors: Dr. Xiaomeng Li, Prof. Andry Rakotonirainy, Prof. Ronald Schroeter, Prof. Sebastien Glaser
